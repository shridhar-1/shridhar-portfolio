Class-D Audio Amplifier

High Efficiency Power Amplifier Project | ECE Dept AITM

üìù Abstract

This project focuses on designing a Class-D Power Amplifier, known for its superior efficiency (up to 90%) compared to traditional Class-A or Class-AB amplifiers. By using Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) to switch transistors strictly ON or OFF, power loss is minimized. The final output is reconstructed using an active Low-Pass Filter (LC Filter).

‚öôÔ∏è Working Principle

1. Modulation: The input audio signal is compared with a high-frequency triangular wave to generate a PWM signal.

2. Amplification: This PWM signal drives the MOSFETs in a push-pull configuration. Since MOSFETs operate only in saturation or cut-off regions, heat dissipation is negligible.

3. Demodulation: A passive LC Low-Pass Filter removes the high-frequency carrier wave, retrieving the amplified audio signal for the speaker.

‚úÖ Conclusion & Results

The prototype successfully amplified low-power audio signals with minimal distortion. Thermal analysis showed significantly lower heat generation than equivalent Class-AB circuits, validating the high-efficiency theoretical model suitable for battery-operated portable devices.
